1. Getting Started

First Time Setup

Meeting Tools is a PWA ‚Äî a Progressive Web App. This means it works like a normal website but can also be installed on your phone or tablet's home screen so it behaves exactly like a native app, even working offline once it's been loaded.

Opening the App for the First Time

1
Open the link in your browser. Safari on iPhone/iPad and Chrome on Android give the best experience.
2
Add it to your Home Screen so it's always one tap away. On iPhone: tap the Share button (the box with an arrow) ‚Üí tap "Add to Home Screen." On Android: tap the three-dot menu ‚Üí "Add to Home Screen" or "Install App."
3
Set your club name. On the main screen, tap the title area where it says "Tap to set club name." Type your club name and tap OK. It will appear every time you open the app.
4
Add your OpenAI API key (optional but recommended ‚Äî enables AI features). See Section 2 below for full instructions.
5
Build your Speaker Roster. Go to Timer or Ah-Counter ‚Üí tap any "Start" option ‚Üí type a name in the search box and tap "Add." Repeat for all your regular members. This roster is saved permanently and you only need to do this once.
üí° Tip All your data ‚Äî roster, settings, logs ‚Äî is saved automatically on your device. Nothing is stored on a server. You won't lose anything if you close the app, but if you clear your browser data you will lose it. Use the Backup Data feature in Settings regularly to keep a safe copy.

Understanding the Two Modes

At the top of the main screen you'll see two tabs: Timer and Ah-Counter. These are the two core tools in the app. You can switch between them at any time from the main screen.

  • Timer ‚Äî Used by the meeting Timer role. Tracks how long a speaker has been talking and signals with color when they hit their time limits.
  • Ah-Counter ‚Äî Used by the Ah-Counter or Grammarian role. Tracks filler words, hosts the Word of the Day, and includes the speech recording/transcription feature.

2. Setting Up Your OpenAI API Key

Required for AI Features

Several features in this app ‚Äî the Word of the Day definitions and the speech transcription ‚Äî use OpenAI's AI services. To use these features, you need to provide your own OpenAI API key. This takes about 5 minutes to set up and costs almost nothing to use.

Why do I need my own key? The app does not have a shared key built in. This is intentional ‚Äî it keeps your meeting data completely private, and it means you control your own usage and costs. OpenAI charges a tiny fraction of a cent per use, so even heavy use at a Toastmasters meeting will cost less than a dollar per year.

Step-by-Step: Getting Your Key

1
Go to platform.openai.com in a web browser. Create a free account if you don't have one, or log in.
2
Click on "API Keys" in the left sidebar. If you don't see the sidebar, look for your account icon or name in the upper right corner and find a menu.
3
Click "+ Create new secret key." Give it a name like "Toastmasters App" so you remember what it's for. Then click "Create secret key."
4
Copy the key immediately. It starts with sk-... and is a long string of letters and numbers. This is the only time OpenAI will show you the full key, so copy it now.
5
Open Meeting Tools ‚Üí tap the ‚öô Settings icon in the top right corner of the main screen.
6
Paste your key into the "ChatGPT API Key" field and tap outside the field to save. The app will remember it from now on.
‚ö† Add a spending limit In your OpenAI account, go to Settings ‚Üí Billing and set a monthly spending limit of $1 or $2. This guarantees you'll never be surprised by an unexpected charge, even if something goes wrong.

What Happens Without a Key?

The app works fine without an API key ‚Äî the Timer and manual Ah-Counter both work fully. The Word of the Day screen will show the word but won't be able to fetch a definition or usage example. The speech recording/transcription feature will not be available without a key.

3. The Timer

Timing Speeches and Segments

The Timer is used by the person in the Timer role during a Toastmasters meeting. It tracks how long a speaker has been talking and uses color to signal when they've reached their time thresholds ‚Äî without anyone needing to watch a clock.

Starting a Timer

1
Make sure you're on the Timer tab on the main screen. Tap "New Timer."
2
Select the speaker. Type their name in the search box or tap their name from the roster list below. Their name will appear in the search box when selected.
3
Select the speech type from the grid ‚Äî Table Topics, Evaluation, Speech, Ice Breaker, or Custom.
4
Tap START. The timer begins immediately.

The Color Signal System

Once the timer is running, the entire screen background changes color to signal timing milestones. This is visible from across the room.

GREEN
Minimum time reached
YELLOW
Target time reached
RED
Maximum time reached

For example, for a 5-7 minute Speech: the screen turns green at 5 minutes, yellow at 6 minutes, and red at 7 minutes. The speaker should aim to finish between green and yellow.

Time Presets

  • Table Topics ‚Äî 1 to 2 minutes
  • Evaluation ‚Äî 2 to 3 minutes
  • Speech ‚Äî 5 to 7 minutes
  • Ice Breaker ‚Äî 4 to 6 minutes
  • Custom ‚Äî You set the Green, Yellow, and Red thresholds yourself in minutes

Pausing and Saving

  • Tap PAUSE to pause the timer. Tap it again (it will say RESUME) to continue.
  • Tap COMMIT & EXIT when the speech is over. This saves the speaker's name, role, and final time to the Meeting Report and returns you to the main screen. The background resets to normal.
üí° Tip ‚Äî Keep the screen on On most phones, the screen will dim or lock after a minute of no interaction. To prevent this, go to your phone's display settings and temporarily set "Screen Timeout" or "Auto-Lock" to a longer time (or "Never") during meetings.

4. The Ah-Counter

Tracking Filler Words Manually

The Ah-Counter feature lets you manually track how many filler words a speaker uses during their speech. Each time you hear a filler word, you tap its card to add a count. At the end of the speech you save the results, which are added to the Meeting Report.

Starting a Counter Session

1
Switch to the Ah-Counter tab on the main screen. Tap "Start Tracking."
2
Select the speaker by typing their name or tapping from the roster.
3
Select the role from the grid ‚Äî Ah Counter, Grammarian, Speaker, etc. This is the role the speaker is filling, not your own role.
4
Make sure "Record Speech" is toggled OFF if you want to count manually. (See Section 6 for the recording mode.) Tap START.

Using the Tally Grid

You'll see a grid of cards ‚Äî one for each filler word being tracked. The default fillers are: AH, UM, ER, SO, LIKE, YOU KNOW.

  • Tap the card to add 1 to the count. The large number in the middle updates instantly.
  • Tap the ‚àí button (bottom right of the card) to subtract 1 if you tapped by mistake.
  • Tap the ‚úï button (bottom left of the card) to remove that filler from the grid entirely for this session. A confirmation will appear first.
  • If a Word of the Day is active, it will appear at the top of the grid with a gold border.

Saving the Session

When the speech is over, tap SAVE RESULTS. The counts are saved to the Meeting Report. If you already have a saved entry for the same speaker in the same role (for example, if a speaker gave a speech and you want to add their Table Topics count too), the app will automatically merge the counts rather than creating a duplicate entry.

üí° Tip ‚Äî Customizing your filler list To add a new filler word that isn't in the default list, go to Settings and use Backup/Restore to export your data, manually add the word to the fillers array in the JSON file, then import it back. Alternatively, the Word of the Day feature automatically adds the active WOD to the grid.

5. Word of the Day

AI-Powered Vocabulary Feature

The Word of the Day (W.O.D.) feature helps the Grammarian or Word Master role. It can generate a random sophisticated word with an AI-written definition and example sentence, or let you search for a specific word. Once selected, the word is tracked on the Ah-Counter grid so you can count how many times speakers use it correctly.

‚Ñπ Requires an OpenAI API key The AI-powered definitions and examples require your OpenAI key (see Section 2). Without a key, the screen will display the word but won't be able to generate a definition.

Generating a Random Word

From the main Ah-Counter menu, tap Word of the Day. The app automatically loads a random word from a built-in library of over 1,000 sophisticated vocabulary words. If you have an API key configured, ChatGPT will generate a definition and a public-speaking-themed example sentence within a few seconds. Tap Random to get a different word.

Searching for a Specific Word

If you already know what word you want to use, type it into the search bar at the top of the W.O.D. screen and tap the üîç button. ChatGPT will generate a custom definition and example sentence for any word you enter.

Selecting the Word

Once you're happy with the word, tap Select. This does two things: it saves the word and its full definition to memory for the Meeting Report, and it adds the word to the top of the Ah-Counter tally grid (with a gold border and "WORD OF DAY" label) so you can count its usage during speeches.

üí° Tip Select your Word of the Day at the beginning of the meeting, before you start tracking any speakers. The word will persist throughout the entire meeting session and appear in the final report.

6. Record Speech & Auto-Transcription

AI-Powered Filler Word Detection

This feature lets you record a speaker's speech using your device's microphone. When they finish, OpenAI's Whisper AI transcribes what was said, and the app automatically counts how many times each filler word appeared ‚Äî saving you from having to tap manually during the speech. You can then review the results before they're sent to the counter.

‚ö† Requirements This feature requires an OpenAI API key (see Section 2) and microphone permission on your device. The first time you use it, your browser will ask permission to use the microphone ‚Äî tap "Allow."

How to Use It

1
Switch to Ah-Counter mode on the main screen. Tap "Start Tracking."
2
Select the speaker and role as normal.
3
Turn on the "Record Speech" toggle at the bottom of the Setup screen. It will switch from gray to green. Tap START.
4
Tap "Start Recording" on the Transcription screen when the speaker begins. The status indicator will pulse red and say "RECORDING LIVE." Place your device where it can clearly hear the speaker.
5
Tap "End Speech" when the speaker finishes. The app sends the recording to OpenAI Whisper for transcription. This usually takes 5‚Äì15 seconds depending on speech length and your internet connection.
6
Review the results. The full transcript appears with filler words highlighted ‚Äî red for regular fillers and gold for the Word of the Day. Below the transcript, a summary shows each filler word and how many times it was detected.
7
Tap "Merge to Counter" to send the detected counts to the Ah-Counter screen. From there you can manually adjust any counts if needed, then tap SAVE RESULTS as usual.

Understanding the Transcript View

The transcript is a literal transcription of what was spoken, including filler words as they were heard. Words that match your filler list are highlighted directly in the text so you can see exactly where in the speech they occurred ‚Äî useful context when giving feedback to the speaker.

Tips for Best Results

  • Microphone placement matters. The closer the device is to the speaker, the more accurate the transcription. Sitting at the same table works well. Being across a large room may reduce accuracy.
  • Background noise such as air conditioning, side conversations, or clattering dishes can affect accuracy. Try to minimize it where possible.
  • Whisper is very good at capturing filler words ‚Äî it's specifically designed to transcribe speech literally rather than "cleaning it up." However, very quiet or mumbled speech may be missed.
  • Always review the results before merging. If you notice a count looks wrong, you can adjust it manually on the counter screen after merging.
  • Internet required. The transcription is processed by OpenAI's servers, so you need an active internet connection. If the venue has poor Wi-Fi, consider using your phone's cellular data as a hotspot.

What if Something Goes Wrong?

  • "Microphone Error" ‚Äî Your browser doesn't have permission to use the microphone. Go to your device's Settings ‚Üí Privacy ‚Üí Microphone and make sure your browser is allowed. Then try again.
  • "Network Error" ‚Äî The app couldn't reach OpenAI. Check your internet connection and tap "Try Again."
  • "Transcription Failed" ‚Äî The audio was sent but something went wrong processing it. This can happen with very short recordings (under 1‚Äì2 seconds). Tap "Try Again."
  • API key error ‚Äî If you see an "AI Error" message, your API key may have expired or been entered incorrectly. Go to Settings and re-paste your key.
üí° Tip ‚Äî Manual mode is always available The recording feature is entirely optional. If you prefer to tap manually, simply leave the "Record Speech" toggle off. Both methods save to the same Meeting Report in exactly the same format.

7. Meeting Report

Viewing and Sharing Results

The Meeting Report collects everything recorded during the meeting ‚Äî all timer results and all Ah-Counter/transcription results ‚Äî into a single formatted log that you can copy, share, or read aloud during the meeting wrap-up.

Viewing the Report

From the main screen, tap Meeting Report (in Timer mode) or Counter Report (in Ah-Counter mode). Both go to the same report ‚Äî all data from the meeting is in one place.

The report shows the date at the top, followed by the Word of the Day and its definition (if one was selected), then a table of all recorded sessions with speaker name, role, and result.

Sharing the Report

  • Copy ‚Äî Copies the full report text to your clipboard so you can paste it into an email, Slack message, or meeting notes document.
  • Share ‚Äî Opens your device's native share sheet, letting you send the report via Messages, email, WhatsApp, or any other app on your device.

Markdown Format

If you share reports in Slack, Discord, or similar platforms, turn on Markdown Export in Settings. This formats the report as a table that renders beautifully in those apps. With Markdown off, the report is plain text suitable for email or copying into a Word document.

üí° Tip ‚Äî Share during the meeting Many Ah-Counters share the report immediately after their verbal summary at the end of the meeting. Tapping Share ‚Üí Email lets you send the full record to the club's mailing list in seconds.

8. Settings & Data Management

Customization, Backup, and Reset

Access Settings by tapping the ‚öô icon in the top right corner of the main screen.

Markdown Export Toggle

When turned on, the Meeting Report is formatted as a Markdown table. This renders as a nicely formatted table in Slack, Discord, and most modern email clients. Turn it off for plain text output that works everywhere.

ChatGPT API Key

Paste your OpenAI API key here. The key is stored only on your device and is never sent anywhere except directly to OpenAI when you use an AI feature. See Section 2 for full setup instructions.

Backup Data (Export JSON)

Tap this to download a backup file containing your entire speaker roster, filler word list, club name, and API key. Save this file somewhere safe ‚Äî your email, iCloud, or Google Drive. You can use it to restore the app on a new device or after clearing your browser data.

‚ö† Back up regularly The app stores data in your browser's local storage, which can be cleared if you clear your browser history or cache. Back up your data before doing any browser cleanup.

Restore Data (Import JSON)

Tap this to import a previously saved backup file. This will restore your roster, fillers, club name, and API key exactly as they were when the backup was made. This is also how you sync your data to a new phone or share the roster with a co-timer.

Clear Meeting Data

This resets the current meeting ‚Äî it clears all recorded timer and counter logs and removes the selected Word of the Day. Use this at the start of each meeting to begin fresh. It does not delete your speaker roster, settings, or API key. A confirmation dialog will appear before anything is deleted.

9. Frequently Asked Questions

Common Questions and Answers

Can two people use the app at the same time ‚Äî one as Timer and one as Ah-Counter?

Yes. If two people both have the app open on separate devices, they each record their own data. You can then combine the reports manually, or one person can import the other's backup JSON at the end of the meeting to merge the data. Native real-time sync between devices is not currently supported.

I accidentally tapped the wrong filler word. Can I undo it?

Yes ‚Äî tap the small ‚àí button in the bottom right corner of the card that was tapped by mistake. It subtracts one from the count, down to a minimum of zero.

A speaker is listed in the report twice. How do I fix it?

The app automatically merges entries when the same speaker name and role combination is saved more than once in a session. If you see a duplicate, it means the name was spelled differently the second time (for example, "JOHN SMITH" vs "JOHN"). The simplest fix is to use the roster ‚Äî always tap the speaker's name from the list rather than typing it, so the spelling is always consistent.

The Word of the Day doesn't show a definition ‚Äî it just says "Add OpenAI Key in Settings."

This means an API key hasn't been entered yet. Go to Settings (‚öô), paste your OpenAI key into the ChatGPT API Key field, then return to the W.O.D. screen and tap Random or search again.

The transcription missed some filler words. Why?

Whisper transcribes what it hears, so accuracy depends on audio quality. Common causes of missed words: the device was too far from the speaker, there was significant background noise, or the speaker spoke very quietly or quickly. The transcription is also limited to words that are in your filler list ‚Äî if a speaker uses "right?" as a filler but "RIGHT" isn't in your list, it won't be counted. You can always adjust counts manually on the counter screen before saving.

Does the app work without internet?

Mostly yes. The Timer, manual Ah-Counter, Meeting Report, and all data management features work completely offline once the app has been loaded. The Word of the Day definitions and the speech transcription require an internet connection because they use OpenAI's cloud services.

How do I add a new filler word to the tracking list?

The easiest method: go to Settings ‚Üí Backup Data to export your data as a JSON file. Open the file in any text editor, find the "fillers" section, and add your new word in quotes (e.g., "BASICALLY"). Save the file, then go back to Settings ‚Üí Restore Data and import it. Your new word will appear on the counter grid.

Can I use this at a non-Toastmasters event?

Absolutely. The app is general-purpose enough to be useful at any meeting, class, or event where speech timing and filler word tracking are helpful. The club name, role names, and filler list are all customizable.

Is my data private?

Yes. All data (roster, logs, settings) is stored locally on your device only. When you use AI features (Word of the Day or transcription), the word or audio is sent to OpenAI using your personal API key, under your own OpenAI account's privacy terms. Nothing is sent to any other server.
